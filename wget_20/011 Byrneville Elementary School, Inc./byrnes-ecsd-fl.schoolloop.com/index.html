 Mission Statement

Byrneville Elementary School, Inc. is committed to the
constant improvement of skills and knowledge
to provide an appropriate and safe environment
in which students can develop their academic,
emotional, physical, and social abilities to their fullest extent.

NEWSLETTER

 

Were you and your child happy with their second week Report Card grades? If not, have you asked for a conference? Progress Reports go home Tuesday, February 7. You might not want to wait until then to find out if there is academic and/or citizenship improvement.

 

Martin Luther King Jr. Day is Monday, January 16. School will not be in session.

 

The week of January 23 is a Celebration Week for us. It is School Choice and Literacy Week. Our annual Book Fair is also that week. School Choice Week is very appropriate because charter schools are schools of choice. This is our 15th year as a charter and our contract has been extended ten more years. We have wonderful plans for our Family Literacy Night, Thursday, January 26 from 6:00 -7:00. You will enjoy the activities and dinner will be provided.

Charter School History

Byrneville Elementary School, Inc.
has been a conversion charter
school since July 1, 2002.
The school uses an existing public
school campus belonging to the
Escambia County School District.
The site consists of four acres located
about 50 miles northwest of Pensacola.
The permanent building was erected
in 1940. The campus also has seven
single and one double portable
classrooms.  Students are transported
by bus or family vehicle because
of hazardous walking conditions.

    NOCCS Administrative Team
  Stephen Ajani — Head of School

Steve is a proud Oakland native whose career in education began with teaching second grade in Washington DC. From there he went on to join the founding teams of a number of high performing KIPP and Achievement First schools in the Northeast, serving as teacher, Dean, Principal, and Director of Engagement. Prior to joining NOCCS, Steve worked as the Chief Program Officer for a small network of schools in Memphis, Tennessee. He earned his B.S. in Business Management from Morehouse College and his M.Ed. in Educational Leadership and Supervision from Howard University. Steve feels very fortunate for having had the opportunity to serve with and learn from some of the finest educators in the District of Columbia, New Haven, New York City, and Memphis. Throughout all of his work in school communities, he has found it critically important to engage and ensure that key stakeholders have a voice at the table.
  David Bond — Director of Operations and Enrollment

Originally hired in 2013 to run our capital campaign, David continues to support fundraising while expanding his role to include oversight of facilities, personnel, admissions and communication. Since 1992, David has been employed in Bay Area schools as a teacher, division head, principal and admissions director, first at Archway School in Oakland and then at Windrush School in El Cerrito. He credits six summers working at North Country School / Camp Treetops in Lake Placid, NY with inspiring his career in progressive education. Originally from Cleveland, Ohio, David earned his B.A. in Fine Art from Yale University and his Teaching Credential from San Francisco State. He currently lives in Oakland, CA with his wife and three children, including two NOCCS graduates. He loves to sea-kayak, make home movies of his family's adventures, and challenges anyone to a game of table tennis!
  Christine Gomez — Dean of Students and School Culture

A NOCCS employee since 2011, Christina "Krea" Gomez, brings over 15 years experience in youth development, community building and community based program management. Krea attended CSU, Fresno where she majored in Sociology. Prior to working at NOCCS, she was a Program Manager at the W. Haywood Burns Institute, where she ran the Community Justice Network for Youth (CJNY) a national network of over 140 organizations who work to reduce racial disparities in the juvenile justice system. While at CJNY she provided organizational and technical support to non-profit organizations and justice administrators all over the country as well as as trained hundreds of young people and adults in community organizing. She was also the Program Director for the Center for Young Women's Development in San Francisco and continues to serve as one of their National Training Institute trainers and experts on rites of passage programming for young women. Krea, is also a co-founder of the Families In Transition Program, which is now the Homeless Education Program of the San Francisco Unified School District.
  Cori Belew — Dean of Instruction

A passionate educator with over a decade of classroom teaching experience, Cori focuses on supporting our lead teachers and implementing our Response to Intervention program to ensure the academic success of all students. She has participated in many professional development opportunities including Project GLAD, Fred Jones' behavior management, Responsive Classroom, and Understanding by Design curriculum planning. Cori graduated from UC Santa Cruz in 2003 and moved to Oakland shortly thereafter. She attended Mills College in 2005 to earn her teaching credential and returned to complete her Masters in Teaching in 2008. Growing up, Cori travelled throughout the United States and Western Europe with her parents and two younger brothers. She currently lives near Lake Merritt in Oakland, with her husband, two children and dog. She is a gardener, attempting to become a runner, and an avid canner, baker, and cook. Working at NOCCS brings together many of Cori's educational passions as well as her interests in growing as an educator.


  Melissa Hawkins — Office and Operations Manager

Melissa brings to NOCCS a great "jack of all trades" background working as a designer, accounts payable clerk, museum educator, and more. Previous positions include Research Associate for the Oakland Museum of California and Designer for a number of companies, including Propagate Designs, Vetrazzo, and Counter Productions. Melissa took a year abroad studying product design at Brunel University in London, and earned her B.A. in Industrial Design and Art from San Francisco State University.
  Lehi Victoria Dickey — Office Coordinator

Lehi joined NOCCS in 2015. For the prior 11 years, she worked as the Site Administrator at East Oakland Leadership Academy. Lehi received her B.A. in Liberal Studies from San Francisco State University and her Masters in Public Administration from California State University East Bay. She is currently working on her Ed.D in Educational Leadership/Social Justice. Originally from San Francisco, Lehi has called Oakland home for the past 20 years. She lives with her fiancé and has three grown children. Outside of school, she loves to cook, read and attend sporting events. Lehi is passionate about making a difference in the lives of children.


  LaKisha Brooks — Technology Coordinator

"Key" Brooks has been involved in the technological arena since 1995. During the course of her career Key has held many positions, from QA to designing the architecture of web components to teaching 5 year-olds how to build a website. She enjoys the challenges of the ever-changing advances in development tools, languages, operating systems and hardware.   WELO (We Lead Ours) — Grades 2–8 After School Program

NOCCS partners with We Lead Ours (WELO) to offer after school programming on-site from dismissal to 6 PM each school day. Established in 2010, WELO provides youth with education, sports, leadership, and community service enrichment opportunities. Activities include sports / outdoor play and arts and crafts, as well as supervised homework time. This is a fee-based option for families, with financial assistance available.
